Communication

Computing and Communication

Communication represents a significant facet of modern computing. Computers are able to interact with one another through vast networks of servers and routing devices using the World Wide Web. Even within homes and offices, computers and computer devices interact with each other through local area networks.

Applications of Digital Communication

Digital communication over the World Wide Web enabled the rise of the modern internet, permitting the creation of online markets, social media platforms, and sites for the dissemination of information. Through communication over the internet, the common computer user is able to easily and conveniently buy and sell assets either material or digital. Simultaneously an individual may interact with others instantaniously regardless of distance using a social media app or site. A user also has immediate access to virtually any information they may want or need, through question and answer boards or wiki sites.

Through the Network

Digital devices sharing a network are all in constant communication with one another. Within a local area network, in say an office, computers and other devices sharing the network are all in communication with one another. This enables users to perform functions like acessing a printer from a laptop for example. On a broader scale, computers in a wide area network may interact with eachother for say backing files up on to the cloud, connecting to a specific server, or the internet via a network switch.
